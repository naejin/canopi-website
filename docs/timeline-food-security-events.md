# Food Security Crisis Timeline — Final Content (12 events)

> **Purpose:** Factual timeline for the Canopi homepage hero section.
> Neutral tone — let the visitor connect the dots.
> Each event: headline + 1-2 hard stats. All stats fact-checked.

---

## Events

### 1973 — Oil Embargo & Food Price Spiral
- OPEC embargo quadruples oil prices — from $3 to $12/barrel in under 3 months.
- Wheat prices triple by early 1974. Soviet Union secretly buys 440 million bushels of US wheat, draining global stocks.

> Sources: EIA, Federal Reserve History, USDA ERS, Brookings Institution

### 1983-1985 — Ethiopian Famine
- Drought and civil war cause worst famine in a century.
- 7.75 million people affected. An estimated 1 million deaths.

> Sources: UN, World Vision, UK House of Lords Library

### 2007-2008 — Global Food Price Crisis
- Rice prices rise 217%, wheat 136%, corn 125% between 2006 and 2008.
- Food riots erupt across 37 countries. India and Vietnam ban rice exports, triggering further panic.

> Sources: FAO, World Bank, USDA

### 2010 — Russian Wheat Export Ban
- Record heatwave and wildfires destroy one-third of Russia's wheat harvest.
- Wheat prices surge 42% in July alone — biggest monthly spike in half a century. Moscow bans grain exports for a year.

> Sources: IMF, USDA ERS, Climate Diplomacy

### 2010-2011 — Arab Spring
- Surging food prices help ignite uprisings across the Middle East and North Africa.
- FAO Food Price Index hits its highest level on record. The MENA region imports one-third of the world's wheat.

> Sources: FAO, Polytechnique Insights

### 2011 — East Africa Famine
- Worst drought in 60 years across the Horn of Africa.
- 260,000 dead in Somalia alone. More than half were children under five.

> Sources: UN, NASA Earth Observatory

### 2019-2020 — East Africa Locust Swarms
- Worst desert locust outbreak in decades ravages cropland across the Horn of Africa.
- Over 25 million people face severe acute food insecurity across the region.

> Sources: FAO, World Bank, WFP

### 2020-2021 — COVID-19 Pandemic
- Lockdowns and economic collapse disrupt global food supply chains.
- People facing acute hunger nearly doubles: from 135 million (2019) to 265 million (2020).

> Sources: WFP, Global Report on Food Crises

### 2022 — Russia-Ukraine War
- Invasion chokes global grain and fertilizer supply. Ukrainian grain exports fall over 90%.
- Russia + Ukraine = 28% of global wheat exports, 75% of sunflower oil. FAO Food Price Index hits a new all-time high.

> Sources: FAO, USDA, UN

### 2023 — India Rice Export Ban
- World's largest rice exporter — 40% of global rice trade — bans non-basmati white rice exports.
- Benchmark rice prices rise 22%. Highest price level in over a decade.

> Sources: USDA Foreign Agricultural Service, PMC/NIH

### 2023-2024 — Red Sea Shipping Crisis
- Houthi attacks force rerouting of global shipping; Suez Canal trade volume drops 42%.
- Container traffic through the canal falls up to 90%. Rerouting via Cape of Good Hope adds 10+ days per voyage.

> Sources: UN, Atlas Institute, Congressional Research Service

### 2026 — Strait of Hormuz Crisis
- Military conflict closes the world's most critical energy chokepoint.
- 20% of global oil, 20% of global LNG trade, and 30% of internationally traded fertilizers transit the strait. Japan depends on it for 94% of its crude oil imports.

> Sources: U.S. Energy Information Administration (EIA), IEA, Nippon.com

## UX & Design Notes

**Placement:** Full-bleed horizontal strip between the hero section (screenshots + download) and the features section.

**Visual style:** Dark charcoal background, light text — wire service / newspaper archive feel. Contrasts with the warm parchment of the rest of the site. Thin timeline axis with event markers.

**Interaction:**
- Horizontal scroll (drag or scroll wheel) on desktop; swipe on mobile
- Events spaced **proportionally to real time** — the acceleration is visually obvious
- Click/tap an event marker to expand a card with headline + stats
- Timeline oriented left (past) → right (present); most recent events visible first

**Mobile:** Horizontal swipe with narrower cards. Natural touch carousel behavior.

**No editorializing.** No call-to-action on the timeline. Proximity to the product does the work.
